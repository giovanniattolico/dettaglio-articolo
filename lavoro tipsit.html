Millions experienced severe disruption as record-breaking winds from Storm Eunice caused death and injury, huge structural damage. Transport chaos nationwide stopped our nation.

Four people were confirmed to have died in the UK and Ireland by Friday evening. The Met Office said a wind speed of up to 122mph was recorded at The Needles on the Isle of Wight on Friday, provisionally the highest ever in England, and described the storm as “the worst since Burns’ Day storm 32 years ago” in which 47 people died.

About a third of UK population - about 20 million people - were told to stay at home as Met Office imposed rare red warnings for much of southern England, Wales and London. The disruption closed thousands of schools on business until about 435,000 homes were left without power early on Friday evening. Hundreds of train services flights were cancelled major roads closed.
